Some of My Favourite Skateparks:

Underpass Park

Underpass Park is Toronto's most distinctive and beloved skate spot, situated beneath the Eastern Avenue overpass in the heart of downtown. The park, established in 2012, has turned a forsaken space into a thriving skater, BMX, and city artist community. It's a combination of ledges, rails, banks, and manual pads, offering the ideal setup for street skating. What makes Underpass Park special is that it can be accessed regardless of the weather, since the highway above provides a shelter from rain and snow. It also serves as a cultural meeting point, with lively graffiti murals adding to its downtown appeal. This makes it a must-visit for skaters in Toronto.

Ashbridges Bay Skatepark

Ashbridges Bay Skatepark, or The Beaches Skatepark as it's more popularly referred to, is one of Toronto's biggest and best-designed skateparks. Located close to Woodbine Beach, the huge skatepark occupies more than 21,000 square feet and boasts a diverse array of features for skaters of all skill levels. The skatepark has bowl areas, banks, rails, stairs, ledges, and a beautiful flow section, making it a perfect haven for street skaters and transition skaters alike. Its picturesque waterfront setting offers a stunning background, and the sharp concrete design guarantees an awesome skating experience. Being a favorite spot for both local skaters and competitive events.

CJ's Skatepark

CJ's Skatepark is Canada's biggest indoor skatepark, located in Mississauga, just outside of Toronto. This massive, skater-owned, not-for-profit park features more than 50,000 square feet of space, carefully designed for skaters of all levels. With its collection of street-style features, bowls, vert ramps, mini ramps, and foam pits, it has become one of the top places for both beginner and advanced riders. CJ's is not only a skatepark but a bustling community center that provides lessons, camps, competitions, and adaptive skate programs. As a year-round facility with a strong emphasis on youth development, CJ's Skatepark is instrumental in the growth of Toronto's skateboarding community.
